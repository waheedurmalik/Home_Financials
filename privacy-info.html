Privacy & Data Information

What happens to your data when you import bank statements

Home Financials uses Google Gemini AI to read and extract transactions from your bank statements and screenshots. This page explains exactly what data is sent, when, what protections are in place, and what the real-world risks are — so you can make an informed choice.

🤖 What is Google Gemini?

Gemini is Google's AI model, similar to ChatGPT. When you import a statement, your app sends the document to Gemini's API via a secure server. Gemini reads the content and returns a list of transactions.

According to Google's current API terms, data sent via the API is not used to train Gemini's AI models. However, Google's policies could change in the future — and even under current terms, other risks exist, which are explained in the next section.

⚠️ Policy change risk: This app cannot automatically detect if Google updates its terms. We recommend checking Google's API terms periodically, especially before importing sensitive documents. The information on this page was last reviewed April 2026.

Why is sending data to Gemini a risk — even if it does not train on it?

This is a fair question. Even if your data is not used to train an AI model, sending personal financial information to any external service carries real-world risks. Here is what could realistically happen:

  • 🔓
    Data breach at Google's end. No company — including Google — is immune to security incidents. If Google's systems were ever compromised, your statement data, including transaction history and personal details, could be exposed to third parties.
  • 👁️
    Human review for safety purposes. Google may review API inputs to detect abuse, ensure safety, or improve service quality. Even if rare, this means your document could in principle be seen by a person at Google — not just processed by a machine.
  • 📋
    Temporary logging. API requests are typically logged for a period of time, even if not stored permanently. Your data exists in Google's systems for some time after the call, even if it is eventually deleted.
  • 🔄
    Policy changes. Google currently states they do not train on API data. But terms can change. If they do, this app would have no way of knowing immediately — and the approach to data submitted under previous terms may be affected.
  • 🌍
    Cross-border data transfer. Gemini processes data on Google's servers, which may be located outside the UAE or UK. Depending on where you live, sending financial data internationally may have implications under local data protection laws.
  • 🏦
    Financial data is highly sensitive. Bank statements are among the most sensitive documents you own. Even if the probability of any one of the above events is low, the consequences of exposure are high — which is why we flag these risks clearly rather than hiding them.
💡 The practical risk for most users is low. But it is real, and you deserve to know about it. The XLS spreadsheet import option avoids all of this entirely — nothing is sent externally.

PDF Statement — Text Mode LOWER RISK
Standard mode for most downloadable PDFs
Most bank PDFs downloaded from online banking work in this mode. Text is extracted on our server first, then sensitive values are removed (scrubbed) before anything is passed to Gemini.
1
Your PDF is uploaded to our secure server — nothing goes to Gemini yet
2
Text is extracted from the PDF
3
Sensitive values are automatically removed (scrubbed) from the text
4
The scrubbed text is sent to Gemini, which returns a transaction list

What gets scrubbed (removed before Gemini sees it):

  • Full account numbers (8–13 digits)
  • IBAN numbers
  • Card numbers
  • Sort codes
  • Phone numbers
  • Email addresses
  • Emirates ID numbers
  • PO Box numbers

What is NOT scrubbed:

  • Your full name (appears in statement header)
  • Branch address (appears in statement header)
⚠️ Your name and branch address may appear in the text sent to Gemini. The risks described above still apply — but are reduced, because your most sensitive financial identifiers have been stripped out first.
🖼️
PDF Statement — Image Mode HIGHER RISK
Triggered when PDF cannot be read as text (scanned documents)
⚠️ This mode is triggered automatically when your PDF is a scanned image rather than a digital document. No scrubbing is possible — the full PDF is sent to Gemini exactly as-is.
1
Your PDF is uploaded — text extraction is attempted but fails (image-only document)
2
The raw PDF file is sent directly to Gemini — no scrubbing is possible
3
Gemini reads the scanned images and returns a transaction list
Sent to GeminiEverything visible in the PDF — your name, address, account numbers, balances, and all transaction details
ScrubbingNone — not possible for image documents
AlternativeUse a digital PDF from your bank's online portal, or export an XLS spreadsheet instead

Why is this higher risk?

All of the risks described above apply — but with your complete, unfiltered statement being sent. There is no scrubbing layer. If Google's systems were ever breached, or if a human reviewed your API call, they would see your full account details and transaction history.

💡 Tip: Most banks offer a "Download Statement" option in online banking that produces a text-based digital PDF (lower risk mode). Scanned paper statements produce image PDFs. When in doubt, use the XLS export instead — it does not use Gemini at all.
📸
Screenshot Import HIGHER RISK
Photos or screenshots of your bank app or statements
⚠️ Screenshots are raw images. Everything visible in the photo is sent to Gemini — no scrubbing is possible for images.
1
You select one or more screenshots from your device
2
Each image is sent directly to Gemini — no scrubbing
3
Gemini reads the visible content and returns a transaction list
Sent to GeminiEverything visible in the screenshot — account numbers, balances, names, and any other on-screen information
ScrubbingNone — not possible for images
AlternativeUse a downloaded PDF or XLS spreadsheet from your bank's online portal

Why is this higher risk?

Screenshots typically show whatever is on your screen at the time — which may include your full account number, current balance, recent transactions, and your name. All of this goes to Gemini unfiltered. All of the risks described above apply in full.

General principles

1
No data stored by us: Home Financials does not operate its own database servers. Your transactions are saved to your own Firebase account (Google Cloud) — not accessible to anyone else.
2
Gemini API — current policy: According to Google's API terms as of April 2026, data sent via the API is not used to train AI models. We recommend checking Google's current terms periodically, as these may change without notice.
3
Your choice: You decide what to import and how. If you are not comfortable sending a document to Gemini, use XLS import or add transactions manually. The app works fully without Gemini.
4
Secure transit: All data is transmitted over HTTPS. The Gemini API key is stored securely on our server — it is never exposed in the app code.
5
Staying up to date: This page was last reviewed in April 2026. If Google's policies change materially, we will aim to update this page. However, we are a personal app and cannot guarantee immediate updates. When in doubt, check Google's terms directly.
